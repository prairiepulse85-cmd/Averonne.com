Research Areas

Academic Research & Methodological Advisory

We collaborate with researchers, academic institutions, and global organisations on project-based engagements, providing structured methodological support and India-based field research coordination.

  • Research architecture and survey framework design tailored to project objectives
  • Statistical and econometric advisory with model validation support
  • Qualitative field research including interviews, focus groups, and on-ground data collection in India
  • Publication-oriented analytical review and reviewer-response structuring

Policy Research & Social Sciences

We bring structured social science methodology to complex policy questions, with particular expertise in social exclusion, inclusive policy, and community-based research in the Indian context.

  • Social exclusion and inclusion policy analysis with intersectional frameworks
  • Community-based participatory research and stakeholder engagement
  • Multi-site field research coordination across Indian states
  • Policy briefs, executive summaries, and stakeholder reporting

Survey Design & Data Collection

We design, pilot, and implement structured data collection instruments for academic and applied research mandates.

  • Digital and telephonic survey instruments designed and piloted to field-ready standards
  • Mixed-methods data collection combining quantitative surveys with qualitative interviews
  • Data quality assurance, codebook development, and dataset preparation
  • Structured findings reports with visual data representation

Malka Zehra

Social Sciences Research Lead
PhD Candidate — Social Exclusion and Inclusive Policy

Malka Zehra is a doctoral researcher at Jamia Millia Islamia, currently pursuing her PhD in Social Exclusion and Inclusive Policy. She holds a Gold Medal in her MA from the same institution and brings structured research experience spanning international development organisations, academic fieldwork, and publication support.

At the International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (CIMMYT), she led the design and implementation of a digital telephonic survey instrument deployed across 350 respondents, delivered qualitative insights to project teams, and authored a comprehensive report on on-farm extension activities. She has additionally contributed research and analytical support to a peer-reviewed academic publication in political sociology.

Her methodological expertise spans both quantitative and qualitative approaches, with particular depth in survey instrument design, field research coordination, and social science research in the Indian context. She is fluent in English, Hindi, and Urdu.
